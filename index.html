Undergraduate Honours Research • Propulsion Development

NERVA-ONE Rocket Engine

NERVA-ONE is a 1 kN regeneratively cooled ethanol-70 / nitrous oxide liquid bipropellant rocket engine developed by Oliver Nicholson and Jake Mackenzie Wood under the supervision of A/Prof Fatemeh Salehi, as part of undergraduate honours research at Macquarie University. The project brings together propulsion analysis, thermal-fluid modelling, structural design, additive manufacturing constraints, and practical engine integration into a single manufacturable propulsion system.

The engine was developed not only as a theoretical design exercise, but as a physical research platform for future validation testing. A major focus of the work was understanding how the coupled nature of cooling channel geometry, pressure drop, wall temperature, material strength, and manufacturing limitations can be managed through a custom computational optimisation workflow.

Material Selection

Thrust Chamber

A major focus of the research is the optimisation of regenerative cooling channel geometry throughout the thrust chamber. Initial thermal estimates are obtained using a Bartz based approximation, followed by a coolant driven search method to determine suitable channel geometries along the chamber length.

To support this process, a one-dimensional computational model has been developed which can generate regenerative cooling channel geometries parametrically while accounting for both the geometric constraints of the engine and the manufacturing limitations of Selective Laser Melting (SLM).

Rather than relying on manually designed cooling layouts, this tool allows rapid exploration of a much broader range of channel configurations and geometric strategies. This enables the investigation of cooling geometries that are often overlooked in student-scale propulsion projects due to design complexity and iteration time.

By integrating computational optimisation with additive manufacturing constraints, the project aims to push the design space of small regeneratively cooled engines further than is typically achievable through conventional design approaches.

Computational Workflow

Simplified workflow of the custom one-dimensional optimisation model used to iteratively solve regenerative cooling geometry, pressure drop, thermal loading, and structural constraints along the engine.

Injector and Manifold

The injector system uses a triplet unlike-impinging injector configuration, designed to balance resultant momentum and promote efficient atomisation prior to combustion. The injector incorporates 20 nitrous oxide injection ports at 1 mm diameter, 10 ethanol injection ports at 0.6 mm diameter, and 10 film-cooling ports at 0.4 mm diameter.

Film cooling accounts for approximately 5% of the total system mass flow rate and provides the primary thermal protection along the chamber wall, significantly reducing local heat flux within the combustion chamber.

The manifold and thrust chamber are additively manufactured in AlSi10Mg using Selective Laser Melting, while the injector itself is machined from brass to achieve the required tolerances for the small injection orifices. Sealing between the injector and combustion chamber is achieved using a graphite-reinforced steel gasket to accommodate the dissimilar materials and operating pressures within the assembly.

Future Work

Further development will focus on expanding both the propulsion platform and the parametric design tools established throughout this research.

Green Propellant Alternatives

Investigate self-pressurising fuel alternatives to replace ethanol, reducing feed system complexity while improving mass, cost, and integration efficiency.

User-Facing Cooling Tool

Develop an interface for the cooling optimisation code so key engine parameters can be modified without directly editing the underlying Python codebase.

Parametric Chamber Generation

Expand automated thrust chamber generation with selectable cooling strategies, helix angles, pressure drop limits, thermal constraints, and manufacturing rules.

Bifurcated Channel Study

Compare bifurcated and non-bifurcated cooling channel geometries to evaluate thermal performance, pressure drop, and manufacturability trade-offs.

Monolithic SLM Architecture

Transition toward a single-part SLM-manufactured engine architecture to reduce assembly complexity and enable more advanced internal cooling geometries.

Student Research Platform

Expand NERVA-ONE into a multidisciplinary framework for future student thesis work across propulsion, thermal analysis, manufacturing, and systems engineering.

Mechanical Design • DFMA • Mechanisms

Modular 3-Stage Planetary Gearbox

A modular three-stage planetary gearbox developed as part of a competitive mechanical design project. The system was designed to lift a prescribed load within a target time while balancing torque transmission, manufacturability, assembly simplicity, and prototype reliability.

The gearbox achieved a total reduction ratio of 47:1 through a compact three-stage planetary arrangement designed to operate within strict geometric and material constraints while delivering the required output torque for lifting a 25 kg load (~110 Nm). A major focus of the project was balancing mechanical performance with practical fabrication methods. The final assembly incorporated both CNC-machined and additively manufactured components, alongside detailed shaft, bearing, and gear train analysis to ensure reliable operation under high loading conditions.
